F.A.Q.s


What is NostraJAMus?

NostraJAMus is an online platform for music discovery, prediction, and tastemaking. How’s that for a vision statement - they’re supposed to be vague and grand, right? NostraJAMus is fantasy sports for music. Choose a song, watch it succeed, reap the benefits. NostraJAMus is a home for music discoverers, explorers, and spelunkers. We incentivize you to discover hidden gems and unheralded releases through our song-picking contests.

What are these ‘contests’?

Exactly what they sound like. These are contests to gauge what songs are ‘getting big’ or ‘blowing up.’ We’re concerned with the playcount increase during the contest timeframe, but we’re also concerned with the artist’s popularity. So we’re way more impressed when you just know that Børns (Who? Exactly.) will get to 5 million plays on 'Electric Love' than when you know Taylor Swift will get there on 'Blank Space.’

Where should I discover new music to submit to these contests?

Everywhere. Music blogs, artist Facebook pages. YouTube, Spotify, SoundCloud, Pandora. Friends, grandmothers, the loudspeakers in your nearest coffee shop. And we’ll be sharing these discoveries with the entire community on the site, creating an infinite cycle of discovery or maybe just an infinite loop.

What can I win?

Cash is king, so we’ll do the kingly thing by handing out cash to our winners. But we also want to foster listener/artist interaction, so we’ll be awarding more musical prizes. That DJ RatPrince guy you submitted in our contest is playing a show near you next week? We’ll make sure to get you tickets if your submission does well. Or a concert poster. Let’s support smaller artists and let’s get DJ RatPrince as the opener on U2’s next tour.

What is a Jam Point?

It’s our way of measuring how your song’s popularity changes over the life of the contest. We love when songs grow by leaps and bounds (so we take percentage increase into account) but we much less love when the song’s artist is a wildly popular power pumped with marketing muscle of a big record label.

Why can I only submit SoundCloud links?

They provide us the easiest data to track fairly for all contestants. We’re working on adding other types of links for contest submissions.

What are groups?

As of now, they’re under construction. We’ll give you a heads up when we’re ready to launch this feature. We think sharing music with friends is dope. It’s that simple. But the process to share music isn’t that simple today. There’s a Spotify inbox and Facebook groups and SoundCloud feeds and good ol’ email and maybe even a burned CD here and there. Our group function is just a closed feed for you and your friends to share one track a day with a brief description, then we’ll aggregate the shared tracks and create playlists for your group. You can start private contests within the group as well.

Why do you have such a stupid name? And why are three of the letters capitalized?

The 16th century French clairvoyant Nostradamus famously predicted the rise of the Beatles, Led Zeppelin, and dubstep. He inspires us daily. Each member of our team has a lifesize cardboard cutout of his handsome figure. Also we thought his name conveyed the notion of prediction. JAM is capitalized because it’s an acronym of course. We can’t tell you (yet) what that acronym means, though. Sorry about that.

Scoring

The Basics

Your song wins Jam Points based on playcount and artist popularity. The song with the most Jam Points at the end of the contest wins.

The best songs begin with fewer streams and are by lesser-known artists. Balancing artist popularity with play counts will be important. Like deciding between a donut and a bagel. We have our best people working on the ideal balance, but for now just pick your favorite jam.
